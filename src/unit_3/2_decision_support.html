Decision support tools

Approaching urban challenges with indicators, dashboards, and decision support

Smart City’s brain

The Back-end

The heuristics, logic, and models.

The Front-end

Dashboards, visualizations, and stakeholder apps.

GOAL

To reduce the “Cognitive Load”

without oversimplifying the “Wicked” reality.

  • Remind them of the “Brain” analogy. A brain is useless if it can’t move the body.
  • Decision Support Tools are the nervous system. They take the data from our models and present it so a Mayor, a Planner, or a Citizen can say “If we do X, then Y happens.”
  • Crucial point: A good DST doesn’t hide complexity; it manages it.

Dashboard’s Anathomy

Indicators

The “Vital Signs” of the city (Air quality, traffic flow, energy use).

“What-If” Engine

Moving from static data to interactive simulation.

Contextual Narratives

Data is meaningless without the “Story” of the place.

  • Refer to your Data Viz notes: “Identifying patterns, trends, and outliers.”
  • In an urban context, a dashboard shouldn’t just be a “report card” of the past. It should be a “flight simulator” for the future.
  • Example: A dashboard that shows current CO2 levels is a viz; a dashboard that lets you “delete” a parking lot and see the CO2 drop in real-time is a Decision Support Tool.

The “Misleading” Interface

Aesthetic Trap

Just because a dashboard is “shiny” or 3D doesn’t mean it’s accurate.

Truncated Realities

What is not being measured? (e.g., social capital, happiness).

Cognitive Biases

How “Visual Narratives” can accidentally (or intentionally) nudge decisions the wrong way.

  • Connect this to your Viz Pitfalls: “Misleading graphs, improper scaling.”
  • In Smart Cities, there is a risk of “Technological Solutionism.” If it’s on a high-res screen, people tend to believe it’s “The Truth.”
  • As curators of these tools, the students must ensure the visualization doesn’t lie about the model’s uncertainty.

Who is the Pilot?

High-Level Ops

Real-time management (Emergency response, traffic control).

Strategic Planning

10–20 year horizons (Housing, Climate adaptation).

Public Participation

Transparency and “Co-design” with citizens.

  • Different users need different “Front-ends.”
  • A technician needs a “Technical” viz. A citizen needs a “Human-centric” viz.
